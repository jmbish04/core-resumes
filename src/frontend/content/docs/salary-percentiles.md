# Understanding Salary Percentiles

Every salary chart in the Salary Intelligence and Salary Data surfaces is built on
**percentiles** — P25, Median (P50), and P75. This page explains what they mean, why
percentiles beat averages, and how to use them when evaluating or negotiating an offer.

## What a percentile actually is

A percentile tells you where a value sits inside a sorted distribution of comparable
salaries. If a salary is at the **75th percentile (P75)**, then 75% of comparable roles pay
less and only 25% pay more.

The three bands shown throughout the app:

- **P25 — 25th percentile.** A quarter of the market is paid at or below this number. It is
  the practical *floor* of a fair range. An offer landing here is usually negotiable upward.
- **Median — 50th percentile (P50).** The exact middle of the market: half of comparable
  roles pay more and half pay less. This is the single most reliable anchor.
- **P75 — 75th percentile.** Only a quarter of the market is paid above this. A strong,
  competitive offer. Reaching P75 typically requires leverage.

## Why percentiles, not averages

A simple average (mean) is easily distorted by a handful of extreme values. One founder-level
package or one underpaid intern can drag the mean far away from what a typical person earns.

The **median** is robust: it does not move when an outlier is added at either tail, because it
only cares about the middle of the sorted list. That is why every headline number in this app
is a median rather than a mean.

> Rule of thumb: if someone quotes you an "average salary," ask whether it is the mean or the
> median. For compensation, the median is almost always the more honest figure.

## Reading the spread

The distance between P25 and P75 — the **interquartile range** — is as informative as the
median itself:

- **Wide spread** → pay is highly variable for this role. Title, company tier, and
  negotiation skill swing the number a lot. The stakes of negotiating well are high.
- **Narrow spread** → the market is tightly clustered. There is little room to move, and an
  offer near the median is likely close to the best you will get.

## How the app computes these

Percentile bands are aggregated from market salary snapshots, weighted by each data point's
**sample size**, then grouped by market segment (remote, local market, top hubs, national) and
by seniority. A larger sample size means a more confident band; the benchmark battery discounts
findings backed by thin data.

## Using percentiles in a negotiation

1. **Locate the offer** against the band for your role, level, and market segment.
2. **Below P25?** You have a strong, data-backed case to ask for more — anchor to the median.
3. **Around the median?** Fair. Push toward P75 using concrete leverage (competing offer,
   scarce skills, expanded scope).
4. **At or above P75?** Already top-quartile. Focus remaining negotiation on equity, sign-on,
   and non-cash terms rather than base.
